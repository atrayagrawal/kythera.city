The idea

This workshop brings parents and children together to create simple musical instruments using everyday and recycled materials. Through music and art, we aim to build connection, shared experiences, and meaningful conversations.

It offers a space for families to see each other in a new light while building something together.

You can carry the experience, along with these small instruments, back home and recreate it, helping you relive memories and discover music together!

Workshop Flow
0 – 45 minutes
Building the Instrument

We will begin with a brief introduction, after which each parent-child pair will create musical instruments using materials like pebbles, twigs, leaves, and other available scraps, decorating them creatively together. The process is designed to be playful and collaborative, with guided prompts to encourage meaningful conversations as they build and explore creativity side by side.

pebbles & mud create instruments decoration foraged from site

45 – 90 minutes
Kythera Jam

In the second part of the session, we will come together for a group jam, sitting in a circle. We will briefly explore beats, rhythm, and voice and how these can come together to create a shared musical experience. Starting with simple rhythms, we will gradually move into rhymes, songs, and expression, with each participant playing the instrument they’ve created, coming together to create a collective and joyful sound.

Atray has been building music communities in Jaipur for over a decade. He leads the weekly Kythera Jams, which are open, free, and participative circles where anyone can explore and discover music within themselves. Vidushi is a child mental health practitioner and social worker who works at the intersection of childhood, art, and well-being.
